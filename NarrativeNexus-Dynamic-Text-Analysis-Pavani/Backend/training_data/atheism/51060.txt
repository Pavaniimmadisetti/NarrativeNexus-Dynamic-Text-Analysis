Introduction to Atheism

What is Atheism?

Atheism is characterized by an absence of belief in the existence of God. Some atheists go further and believe that God does not exist. The former is referred to as weak atheism, the latter as strong atheism.

Weak atheism is simple skepticism and disbelief in the existence of God. Strong atheism is a positive belief that God does not exist. Some atheists believe in the non-existence of all Gods while others limit their atheism to specific Gods such as the Christian God.

Disbelief and Belief

Disbelieving in a proposition means one does not believe it to be true. Not believing something is true is not equivalent to believing it is false. One may simply have no idea whether it is true or not. This distinction is crucial to understanding atheism.

What is Agnosticism?

The term agnosticism was coined by Professor Huxley at a meeting of the Metaphysical Society in 1876. An agnostic is someone who believes that we do not and cannot know for sure whether God exists. This differs from atheism which addresses belief rather than knowledge.

Philosophical Justifications for Atheism

There are many philosophical justifications for atheism. Many atheists feel that the idea of God as presented by major religions is self-contradictory and logically impossible. Others are atheists through skepticism because they see no evidence that God exists.

Assumptions and Burden of Proof

If we assume that something does not exist, we can show this assumption is invalid by finding a single counter-example. If we assume something exists, showing the assumption is invalid may require an exhaustive search of all possible places where it might be found, which is often impractical.

Therefore it is generally accepted that we must assume things do not exist unless we have evidence that they do. This principle applies universally - even theists do not believe in unicorns despite inability to conclusively prove they don't exist anywhere.

Atheism and Religion

Atheism is not a belief in any sort of superhuman power nor is it categorized by worship. Religion is generally characterized by belief in a superhuman controlling power and by faith and worship. Atheism lacks these defining characteristics.

Some varieties of Buddhism are not religion according to traditional definitions. Similarly, atheism does not qualify as a religious belief system.

Atheism and Faith

Skeptical atheism is something one questions rather than has faith in. Both atheism and science are based on core assumptions subject to questioning if experience contradicts them. If such basic ideas are called acts of faith, then almost everything we know would be based on acts of faith, making the term meaningless.

Atheism and Anti-Religion

Atheism is the position that runs logically counter to theism. However, atheist attitudes towards theists cover a broad spectrum. Most atheists take a live and let live attitude and will not usually mention their atheism except to close friends.

Some atheists are vocal about their beliefs where they see religion encroaching on secular matters like government. Such individuals are concerned that church and state remain separate as established in constitutional law.

Atheism and Morality

If morality is defined as obedience to God, then atheists are less moral by that definition. However morality usually refers to what is acceptable and unacceptable behavior within society. Humans are social animals and must cooperate to succeed, giving atheists reason to discourage immoral behavior for self-preservation.

Many atheists behave morally because they feel natural empathy for other humans. There exist both atheist and religious individuals who behave immorally and attempt to justify their actions through their respective belief systems.

Atheism as Choice

Studies show that over ninety percent of atheists became atheists because religion did not work for them. They found religious beliefs fundamentally incompatible with their observations. Atheists are unbelievers through choice and careful consideration, not ignorance or denial.

Many atheists have spent time studying one or more religions, sometimes in great depth, before making their decision to reject religious beliefs. For naturally skeptical people, atheism often represents the only choice that makes logical sense.
